Kuconomics - the distribution model

Below we describe the tokenomics of KUCO distribution. The model assumes a fixed Uniswap-V2 liquidiy pool, denoted LP, that collects ETH for an initially fixed deposit of newly minted KUCO tokens. Before reading the following scheme, note that you should try to understand the scheme in the positive light, without negative judgement, in the way we want you to.

1. Investing ETH liquidity

The liquidity pool LP has an initial deposit of a billion KUCO tokens, with deposit's liquidity burned. Before KucoCoin can be traded, users can invest in the pool, by providing ETH as liquidity, which is then burned. Investment is nonrefundable.

2. Collecting minted KUCO

After some time, KUCO will be minted to the accounts of investors who deposited ETH to the liquidity pool LP. If at that time the liquidity in LP is comprised of K KUCO and E ETH, with some user investing e ETH, then they will get 1.2 × e × K / E KUCO in return.
Note that in the second step the user gets k KUCO, where k solves the equation k × E / K = 1.2 × e. Because E / K is the price of KUCO in ETH on the liquidity pool, they get 1.2 × e ETH worth of KUCO. We call this scheme the Midas scheme, because it turns your initial investment of Ether shit into KucoCoin gold. It is symbolized by the triangle because famously King Midas was very much into triangles and he really liked them because they were his favorite shape.
